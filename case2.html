Case File No. 12

Last Call at the Mercer

A Downtown Bar Mystery

The Situation

The Mercer's tenth anniversary party wrapped up just after midnight — confetti, the good whiskey, half the neighborhood packed into a bar built for half that many. By 12:30 the doors were locked and the last guests gone.

At 1:15 AM, the doorman found Tom Reyes, the Mercer's owner, dead on the floor of his upstairs office. He'd been struck once with one of his own anniversary trophies — the ten-year award that sat on his shelf for exactly one night before it was used on him.

Five people had keys, reasons, or both to still be in the building after closing: his bartender, his business partner, his ex-wife, his newest investor, and the doorman who found him. Everyone has an account of where they were. Not everyone's account survives contact with someone else's.

Question them. Cross-reference what they tell you. When the timeline stops adding up for one of them, you'll know who to name — but you only get one name.
