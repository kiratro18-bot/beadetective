Case File No. 22

Dead Reckoning

Murder Aboard the MS Solveig

The Situation

Five nights at sea, no ports until landfall, and somewhere around 1:00 AM, Magnus Thorne — shipping magnate, owner of the Solveig, and a man with a gift for making enemies politely — went over the railing of the observation lounge onto the deck below. Two decks up. No witnesses who'll admit to seeing it happen.

The captain's dinner had broken up around midnight. By the time the body was found at 1:15, most of the ship's small circle of people who actually knew Magnus well were somewhere between their cabins and the bar — or so they say.

Known to be aboard and connected to Magnus:
His wife. His captain. His first mate. His ship's doctor. His biographer. His estranged son. His head steward. His security officer. Eight people, one truth, and four days left at sea to find it before landfall makes this someone else's problem.
